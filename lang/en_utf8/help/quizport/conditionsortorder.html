Sort order

The sort-order of pre-conditions serves two purposes. Firstly, it specifies the order in which the pre-conditions are processed. Secondly, it allows pre-conditions to be combined into groups of conditions, any of which can enable access to the quiz.

In the simple case, all pre-conditions have the same sort order, usually "0", and access to the quiz is granted if all the pre-conditions are satisfied.

In a more complicated situation, pre-conditions on a quiz may have different sort-orders. In such a situation, pre-conditions will be grouped together acording to their sort order. There may be more than one pre-condition in any these groups. If all conditions in any group are satisfied then the student is given access to the quiz.

In technical terms, we could say that the sort-order groups on a quiz are joined with logical-OR, whereas the members within a sort-order group are joined with logical-AND.

To illustrate this with an example, consider the following conditions on a quiz

  • sort-order group (1) contains pre-conditions (a), (b) and (c).
  • sort-order group (2) contains pre-conditions (d) and (e)

The pre-conditions are satisfied if (a) AND (b) AND (c) are true, OR if (d) AND (e) are true.
